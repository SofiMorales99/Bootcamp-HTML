Katatonia

Katatonia (Catatonia) is a psychiatric condition often associated with schizophrenia.
It is characterized by a general absence of motor activity or violent, hyperactive behavior.
The word "catatonia" (κατατονία) is also a Greek word for low tone, undertone or commonly down-tempo.


Sweden's Katatonia attempted a fusion of Paradise Lost's doom metal, Cure's manic depression,
Sisters Of Mercy's hallucinated gothic and and late-period Pink Floyd's baroque orchestrations.
Jonas Renkse was not a great vocalist and Anders Nystrom was not a virtuoso guitarist,
but the interplay between the singer's melodrama and the guitarist's huge riffs coined a new dramatic form.
They debuted with the single Jhva Elohim Meth (1992) and jumped to the forefront of the doom movement with the suicidal album Dance of December Souls (1993),
thanks to a sound heavy on the keyboards and derivative in the melodies,
and lengthy songs that took the listener another world (Gateways Of Bereavement, Velvet Thorns, Tomb of Insomnia).
